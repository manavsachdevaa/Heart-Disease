About This Project

Understanding Heart Disease Through Data

Heart disease is a group of diseases related to cardiovascular diseases, manifested by a violation of the normal functioning of the heart — covering the epicardium, pericardium, myocardium, endocardium, valvular apparatus of the heart, and heart vessels.

📌 Project Overview

This platform demonstrates how to use data analytics and interactive visualization to surface actionable insights about cardiovascular health. Using a dataset of over 4,000 patients, we explore multiple risk factors and their correlations with heart disease diagnosis.

According to the National Heart, Lung and Blood Institute in Framingham (USA), the most important factors in the development of cardiovascular disease in humans are obesity, sedentary lifestyle, and smoking.

This analysis validates and expands on those findings using real patient data visualized through Chart.js and presented via a Flask web application.

⚠️ Key Risk Factors Analyzed

🚬

Smoking

Tobacco use significantly increases cardiovascular risk across all demographics.

🍺

Alcohol Consumption

Heavy drinking compounds cardiovascular risk, especially when combined with smoking.

🩸

Diabetes

Diabetic patients show disproportionately higher rates of heart disease in this dataset.

🛋️

Sedentary Lifestyle

Inactive individuals are 2.2× more likely to be diagnosed with heart disease.

🧬

Age & Demographics

Risk peaks in the 65–74 age group and varies significantly across racial groups.

Methodology

How This Analysis Was Built

A structured pipeline from raw data to interactive web visualizations.

📥

Data Collection

Patient data sourced from the Behavioral Risk Factor Surveillance System (BRFSS) dataset.

🧹

Data Cleaning

Removed duplicates, handled null values, and normalized categorical fields for consistency.

📊

Visualization

Charts built with Chart.js — bar, pie, doughnut, and grouped bar charts for multi-variable analysis.

🌐

Web Application

Flask framework powers the backend; Jinja2 templates render dynamic pages with real data.

📖

Story Telling

Guided 6-step narrative walks users through the most important findings in logical sequence.

🔗

Open Source

Full source code available on GitHub for transparency and reproducibility.
